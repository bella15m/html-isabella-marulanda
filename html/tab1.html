EXOPLANETS

They are planets that orbit a star other than the sun and therefore do not belong to the solar system. Extrasolar planets became the object of scientific investigation in the 20th century.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
_TYPES OF EXOPLANETS_
-Jovian: Gas giants that may be larger than Jupiter, with some subtypes, such as the so-called cold Neptune or super-Jupiter.

-Terrestrial: Solid planets, similar to terrestrial planets.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
_FUN FACTS_
-A short time ago, NASA discovered a solar system made up, not by one, but by 7 different planets orbiting around its star.

-The exoplanets with the highest similarity index to planet Earth are: Kepler-296e (93% similarity) and Kepler-395c (91% similarity)

-More than 4,100 exoplanets are known so far, which are very difficult to see directly with telescopes.

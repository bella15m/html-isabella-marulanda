THE TEMPERATURE OF THE STARS


•Stars are currently classified into spectral classes identified using the letters-O-B-A-F-G-K-M-A sequence ranging from the hottest (type 0) to the coldest (type m)

• The cold stars, that is to say, those of spectral types k and M, radiate most of their energy in the red zone in the infrared of the electromagnetic spectrum and that is why they are seen in color , while the hot stars, that is, those of type O and B, emit most of their radiation at wavelengths from blue to ultraviolet, making them appear blue or white

• Blue stars are among the most Hot and the rarest in the universe, their life is relatively short compared to the type of most abundant stars in the universe, red dwarfs, which, being small, can live for hundreds of billions of years
